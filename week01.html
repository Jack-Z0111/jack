Reflection

by Jack Zhang

The appreciation of visual art is developing over times, and the Way of Seeing gives a glance on how people follow the tide in different eras. Before the revolution, real artworks can only be seen in galleries. When camera started to appear in normal life, people found ways to record up the art which would probably disappear on day. At this point, I think some kind of voice started to grow. Cameras, photographs added a value other than appreciation on the visual artwork, that people may need to preserve that precious thing. The way of seeing becomes the way of observing and keeping. While I walk in galleries and see flashlight and lens towards painting, I consider these visitors may not put their full selves into seeing the beauty, which I think is the loss of way of seeing.
